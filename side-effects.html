RepairVite Side Effects: What to Know

A plain-language overview of reported reactions, contraindications, and who should be cautious with Apex Energetics RepairVite (K-60), 173.7 g powder, ~30 servings.

What might you feel? Most people feel nothing major. If you do react, it usually happens in the first week. Here's what to watch for.

Most Commonly Reported Reactions

Across user reports and practitioner observation, the side effects most often associated with RepairVite fall into a few categories:

  • Bloating in week one. This is the most common one. It usually fades in a few days.
  • A headache around days three to five. About one in twenty people. Drink more water.
  • Feeling jittery or anxious from the glutamine. Less common. More likely if you're prone to anxiety or have a seizure history. Start with half a scoop to catch it early.
  • A little constipation. The soothing herbs can slow you down.
  • Symptoms coming back if you quit suddenly. Taper off instead.
  • A rare allergic reaction. Usually the chamomile, if you react to ragweed.

Who Should Be Cautious

Who should not take it? Skip it if you're pregnant. The licorice (DGL) isn't recommended, and it's not labeled for pregnancy. Skip it if you have a seizure history. The big glutamine dose is a concern there. Skip it if you take lithium or any drug with a narrow safe range. Stop and call your provider if you get new belly pain, diarrhea that won't stop, or you can't sleep or settle down. Have a serious gut diagnosis like IBD or celiac? Talk to your GI doctor first. Don't start it on your own.

What to Do If You Experience a Reaction

If a reaction occurs, the standard guidance is to stop the supplement and contact your healthcare provider. A clinician can review the full ingredient list, your other medications and supplements, and any underlying conditions that may be relevant. Drug and Supplement Interactions

Will it clash with your meds? The list is short. On lithium or another narrow-range drug? Check with your prescriber. Licorice compounds can shift your fluids. Take other pills? Keep RepairVite at least thirty minutes apart from them. The soothing herbs can block absorption. On seizure or mood meds? Clear the glutamine with your doctor. Heart or cholesterol meds? No known clash. Either way, just tell your provider what you're taking.

Long-Term Use Considerations

How long do you stay on it? Not forever. Plan on sixty to ninety days. Feeling better at the end? Drop to once a day for another month, then check in and probably stop. Feel nothing by week eight to twelve? Stop it. That usually means your gut barrier wasn't the real problem. There's no reason to take this for years. And it isn't cheap. You can read more about when to taper or stop at a clinician's full review of RepairVite K-60.

Bottom line. Should you take it? If you have a real barrier problem — gut recovery after antibiotics, the window after an elimination diet, or an autoimmune plan — it's a fair add. Start slow for a few days. Then full dose in cool water between meals. Keep it thirty minutes from other pills. Treat it as a sixty-to-ninety-day course. Skip it in pregnancy, with a seizure history, or on lithium. And don't make it your first move if you haven't done the diet work yet.
