RepairVite FAQ

Quick answers to the questions visitors most often ask about Apex Energetics RepairVite (K-60), 173.7 g powder, ~30 servings.

What is this stuff?

You probably got handed a tub by a practitioner. Here's the short version. It's a gut-repair powder. The main ingredient is glutamine, which feeds your gut lining. The rest soothes and supports. You use it for a while, then you stop. I'd read a clinician's full review before you commit.

Will it upset my stomach?

A little, maybe, at first. The most common thing is some bloating in week one. It usually fades. A few people get a headache early on. If you're an anxious type, the glutamine can feel a bit much. Go slow and you'll likely be fine. The side-effects page covers it all.

How do I actually take it?

One scoop, twice a day. Mix it in cool water. Take it between meals, not with food. Start at half a scoop for the first few days. Keep it away from your other pills by half an hour. You're on it for two or three months, not forever.

Is it going to heal my gut?

Be honest with yourself here. It helps when there's a real reason your gut lining is struggling. Like after antibiotics, or during a reset diet. It won't fix vague symptoms with no cause behind them. The glutamine science is decent, not miraculous. Treat it as one tool, not the answer.

What's actually in it?

Glutamine, first and foremost. Then DGL licorice, zinc carnosine, slippery elm, marshmallow root, a touch of chamomile, and quercetin. Nothing hidden in a blend. You can see every dose. The ingredients page spells out what each one does.

Should I get K-60 or the SE one?

Most people start with K-60. That's this one. The SE version (K-98) is stronger. It's for sensitive folks or tougher protocols. Don't guess between them yourself. Ask whoever recommended it. They'll match it to how you handle things.

Is there anyone who shouldn't take it?

Yes. Skip it if you're pregnant. Skip it if you've had seizures. Talk to your doctor first if you take lithium. And stop if you get new belly pain, ongoing diarrhea, or feel really anxious. The clinician's review goes deeper on this.
